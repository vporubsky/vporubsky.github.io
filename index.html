Veronica Porubsky@vporubsky
I am a Bioengineering Ph.D. student working in Dr. Herbert Sauro's lab at the University of Washington to apply graph theory, probabilistic modeling, and control theory to neural recordings in order to understand cellular communication changes during anxiety.

I also serve as the Head of Outreach for the Center for Reproducible Biomedical Modeling; trying to make computational modeling for medical applications more reproducible.

Research.

Inferred functional connectivity patterns of neuronal netoworks to decipher anxiety states
I am using graph theory to study dynamic changes to functional neural connectivity patterns in the dentate gyrus as mice are exposed to anxiogenic and neutral environments during fear conditioning. This study is performed in collaboration with Michael Bruchas' group, using calcium imaging data collected by Eric Zhang.
Probabilistic models of the dentate gyrus during context encoding in a fear conditioning paradigm
Using the graph theory analyses and calcium imaging data from my project deciphering anxiety states, along with data gathered from literature, I am building probabilistic network models to mimic the dynamics found in vivo in order to study the effects of perturbing the network in silico.
Case study in reproducible biomedical modeling
In collaboration with Dr. Herbert Sauro and Dr. Joseph Hellerstein, I am working on an interactive Jupyter notebook-based book chapter to demonstrate a reproducible biochemical modeling workflow.
